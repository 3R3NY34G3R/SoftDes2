Chicken Shawarma: A Street Food Classic

Chicken Shawarma is a beloved Middle Eastern dish known for its bold spices and smoky flavor. Traditionally cooked on a vertical rotisserie, this recipe brings the taste of shawarma to your home kitchen.

"If it's not tight, it's not right." – Sun Tzu, Art of Shawarma

Ingredients:

  • Olive Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Water: 1 tablespoon
  • Lemon Zest: 1 tablespoon
  • Lemon Juice: 1 tablespoon, freshly squeezed
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • Coriander: 2 teaspoons
  • Paprika: 2 teaspoons
  • Cumin: 2 teaspoons
  • Turmeric: 1 teaspoon
  • Cinnamon: ½ teaspoon
  • Ginger: ½ teaspoon
  • Cayenne Pepper: ¼ teaspoon
  • Cardamom: ¼ teaspoon
  • Chicken Thighs: 2 pounds, boneless and skinless
  • Canola Oil: 1 ½ tablespoons
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation:

  1. Whisk olive oil, water, lemon zest, lemon juice, garlic, and spices in a bowl.
  2. Marinate chicken in the mixture for at least 2 hours (up to overnight).
  3. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Brush chicken with canola oil; season with salt and pepper.
  5. Grill chicken until cooked through (internal temperature 165°F), about 10 minutes.

Cooking Tips:

  • Use a cast iron skillet for even heat and a perfect sear.
  • Grill outdoors for smoky, charred flavor.
  • Marinate overnight for maximum taste.
  • Freeze marinated chicken for up to 3 months.
